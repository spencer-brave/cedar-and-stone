Our Thinking

A book read together is the most powerful thing in a child's world

There's a moment, right around page three, when your child stops squirming and leans in. Their breathing slows. They point at something in the illustration and look up at you to make sure you saw it too.

That moment isn't an accident. It's the whole point. Every book we publish is designed to create more of those moments. Moments where a parent and child are completely locked in, together, without trying.

We work with child development advisors, experienced educators, and some of the best illustrators in the world to craft stories that hold attention the right way. Not through overstimulation, but through genuine wonder.

How We Build Books

Stories that work on every level

Beautiful enough to display. Smart enough to matter. Fun enough that you'll catch yourself turning pages after they've fallen asleep.

01

Story First

We start with the story, not the lesson. If the narrative doesn't captivate on its own, nothing else matters. Our writers craft stories that surprise, delight, and stick. Then we layer in the meaningful stuff so naturally you barely notice it's there.

02

Art That Earns the Shelf

We work with illustrators whose work belongs on walls, not just pages. Rich color, real texture, compositions that reward a second and third look. These books don't end up in a donation bin.

03

Moments, Not Just Pages

Spread by spread, we design natural pauses, questions, and interactive beats that pull parent and child into the story together. Not forced. Not labeled. Just woven into the fabric of the reading experience.

Who Makes These Books

Authors and experts who actually get it

Every Cedar & Stone book is a collaboration between an author with something real to say and a team obsessed with getting every detail right: the story, the art, the pacing, the science behind how children connect with a book.

We partner with authors, educators, and storytellers who care as much about your child's bookshelf as you do. Then we pair them with world-class illustrators, child development advisors, and a production team that doesn't cut corners.
